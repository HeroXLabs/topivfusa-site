Acridine Orange Staining

Figures (A), (B) and (C) were the sperm samples stained with Acridine Orange Dye to identify normal (double-stranded DNA) or abnormal (single-stranded DNA) sperm chromatin. This technique was processed under fluorescence microscopy.

  • Figure (A): This sperm sample was collected from a fertile gentleman. There was a 38% sperm head appeared in yellow to red color, i.e., single-stranded and abnormal DNA. The other sperm showed green head, i.e., double-stranded and normal DNA. (Note: Only green-colored sperm head is normal.)

  • Figure (B): This sperm sample was collected from an infertile patient. You can see that almost all of the sperm heads appeared in orange color, i.e., single-stranded and abnormal DNA.

  • Figure (C): Almost all of sperm cells (about 99%) appeared in green color, i.e., double-stranded and normal DNA, following sperm micro-separation via Wang Tube Technology.

We will choose “The Wang Tube Sperm Micro-Separation Technology” as the first choice for your sperm selection. The Top IVF USA takes pride in excellence and is the world leader in this special achievement, which has been greatly contributing to our patients.
